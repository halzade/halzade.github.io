The Velvet Revolution in Czechoslovakia, 1989

Prague

A local Prague legend covers the events at the dam of the Kyjský pond during the days of the 1989' revolution.

In the early morning hours, at the pond’s dam, the revolutionary team led a battle against a senior member of the Communist party and his gang. He defeated, signed the Charter 77.

Here, in the morning hours, the revolution was named! Twenty seconds later, at the same place, Lukas started the historical key-ringing. Hours later, the whole newborn republic joined.

During the day, the dam was re-taken by communist counter-revolutionaries, so the revolutionary team returned there in the afternoon. This time, against a stronger opponent.

Nový Bor, Jižiny

Naturally, something like that isn’t without causes and consequences. Therefore, when the Russian Emperor Michail Gorbachev arrived to discover why he lost Prague and why he’s losing all of Czechoslovakia, he sought after Lukas.

In a personal confrontation with Gorbachev, he was very unhappy about Lukas’s disinterest in being governed by him.

In a battle of words and one-on-one standoff, Gorbachev budged, and stepped aside for a single moment.

Lukas thrust his hands in the air and started shouting: “Here, the revolution is victorious!” The Russian emperor was standing just next to him.

The revolution is named after Lukas's left velvet palm.
He started the historical key-ringing with his right.
